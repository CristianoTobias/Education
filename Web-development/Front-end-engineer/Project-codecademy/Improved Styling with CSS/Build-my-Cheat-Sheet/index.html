<!DOCTYPE html> Doctype tells the browser the version of HTML being used in the document.

<main> </main> Main They represent the main content of its body, that is, the content directly related to the central topic of the page or with the central functionality of the application;

<section> </section> Section These tags define a session for your page;
<article> </article> Article These tags define an article on your page. In this sense, they are used to separate the content of your page. Widely used mainly by blogs or content pages;
<aside> </aside> Aside These tags represent a section of a page whose content is tangentially related to the surrounding content, which could be considered separate from the content;
<nav> </nav> Nav This tag defines navigational content. Therefore, it is widely used in conjunction with lists and in creating menus;
